Consider a larger grid (20 x 20), with one new rule â€“ the game ends after 300 moves or 5 minutes, at which you must begin a new game. The grid has the same action constraints as the previous grid. The first move has been made for you.

You play two games to 300 moves, and reveal the following values.

You have discovered a strategy that will yield 180 points: play all the blue +1 squares for a total of 180 points, avoid the orange -1 squares, and wait for the clock to run out. You can choose to play this strategy or you can choose to play another game to explore the grey squares.

What do you do next: play a known 180 point game, or a game with unknown rewards (but potentially as low as 60 points)?

As it turns out, there is a cluster of blue +1 rewards that had yet to be discovered. The optimal total rewards for this game isn't +180, but +238. This dilemma, between 'exploiting' a known optimal strategy, or 'exploring' further is known as the exploration-exploitation trade-off.
